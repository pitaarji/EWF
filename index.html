The Exlcuded Workers' Fund: Helping Undocumented New Yorkers

Peter Gill

In a historic initiative, the New York State Department of Labor is providing undocumented workers who lost work during the COVID-19 pandemic what they would have been eligible for in unemployment insurance and stimulus checks -- up to $15,600 per worker. To apply, workers must prove their identity, residency, and loss of work.

Applicants can prove their identity with a NYS Driver’s License, NYS non-driver ID card, IDNYC, or a foreign passport in combination with other documents.
Applicants can prove their residency with a NYS Driver’s License, NYS non-driver ID card, IDNYC, NYS learner’s permit, tax return, utility bill, bank statement, or other Documents.
Applicants can prove that they lost work during the pandemic by showing a NYS tax return, a taxpayer identification number, six weeks of pay stubs or wage statements before losing income, a letter from their employer showing the worker’s dates of work and reason for loss of income, or other documents.
To date, 350,823 claims have been submitted to the Department of Labor, of which 126,348 have been accepted. $1.42 billion has been dispersed.
